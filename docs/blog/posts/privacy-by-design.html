Privacy by Design: What We Collect (and What We Don’t)

Published: September 26, 2025 | Last Updated: September 26, 2025

Privacy isn’t a feature—it’s a foundation. The Markdown Link Checker was built from the ground up to respect user autonomy, minimize data exposure, and eliminate hidden behavior. This post outlines exactly what the tool does and doesn’t collect, and why that matters for compliance-conscious users.

What We Collect

The tool processes only what’s necessary to validate links:

  • Markdown content you paste or upload
  • URLs extracted from that content
  • File names (for display and export)
  • Basic technical data (e.g., browser type) for performance monitoring

No personal identifiers are required or requested. You can use the tool anonymously and offline.

What We Don’t Collect

  • No names, emails, or login credentials
  • No behavioral tracking or analytics
  • No server-side storage of your content
  • No cookies beyond essential session handling (if any)

Everything runs client-side. Your data stays in your browser and disappears when you close the tab.

Why It Matters

Many link validators rely on cloud infrastructure, logging your content and behavior for analytics or monetization. This tool takes the opposite approach: stateless, transparent, and respectful of your boundaries. It’s ideal for teams working under GDPR, CCPA, POPIA, or internal privacy policies.

FAQ

Is my content uploaded anywhere?

No. All processing happens locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server.

Do you use cookies?

Only if essential for session handling. No tracking or profiling cookies are used.

Can I request data deletion?

There’s nothing to delete—your data is never stored.
