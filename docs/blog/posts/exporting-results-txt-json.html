Exporting Results: TXT vs JSON

Published: September 26, 2025 | Last Updated: September 26, 2025

Once your Markdown links are validated, you’ll want to act on the results. Our Markdown Link Checker lets you export findings in two formats—TXT and JSON—each suited to different workflows. Whether you're collaborating with editors or integrating into a CI pipeline, the export options are designed to be flexible and frictionless.

TXT Format

TXT exports are simple and human-readable. Each line includes the link text, URL, status, and optional notes. This format is ideal for manual review, editorial checklists, or quick sharing in chat threads and bug reports.

JSON Format

JSON exports are structured and machine-readable. They include full metadata for each link—text, URL, line number, status, and category. This format is perfect for automation, audits, or integration into static site generators and CI workflows.

Use Cases

  • TXT: Editorial review, manual fixes, team feedback
  • JSON: Programmatic ingestion, dashboards, automated reporting
  • Clipboard: Quick copy-paste into GitHub issues or Slack threads

Export Workflow

After validation, use the export buttons to download your results. You can also copy them to your clipboard with a single click. No data is stored or transmitted—everything is generated locally in your browser.

FAQ

Can I export both formats?

Yes. You can download TXT and JSON independently, depending on your workflow.

Is the export stored anywhere?

No. All exports are generated client-side and never sent to a server.

Can I customize the export?

Not yet—but future versions may support column selection, filtering, or custom formatting.
