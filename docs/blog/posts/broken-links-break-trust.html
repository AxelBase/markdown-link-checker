Why Broken Links Break Trust

Published: September 26, 2025 | Last Updated: September 26, 2025

Broken links are more than technical glitches—they’re trust breakers. Whether you're publishing documentation, educational content, or compliance materials, a dead link signals neglect. Our Markdown Link Checker helps you catch these issues before they reach your audience.

Why Link Integrity Matters

Users rely on links to navigate, verify, and explore. A broken link can frustrate readers, damage credibility, and even violate accessibility standards.

Common Causes of Broken Links

  • Moved or deleted pages
  • Typos in URLs
  • Expired redirects
  • Incorrect relative paths

How This Tool Helps

Our validator checks every link in your Markdown file—external, relative, and anchor—and flags issues with clear status codes and context. You can filter, sort, and export results for fast resolution.

FAQ

Can broken links affect SEO?

Yes. Search engines penalize sites with poor link hygiene, which can lower your visibility.

Does this tool check internal links?

Yes, including relative paths and anchor links, using a virtual file tree if configured.

How often should I validate links?

Before every major publish or update. Regular checks prevent stale content and broken navigation.
