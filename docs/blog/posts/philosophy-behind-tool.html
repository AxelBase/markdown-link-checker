The Philosophy Behind the Tool

Published: September 26, 2025 | Last Updated: September 26, 2025

This isn’t just a link checker—it’s a statement. The Markdown Link Checker was built for users who value autonomy, transparency, and reproducibility. It’s designed to serve writers, educators, and compliance professionals who distrust opaque systems and prefer tools that do one thing well—without compromise.

Design Principles

  • Privacy-first: No tracking, no uploads, no hidden analytics
  • Stateless: Everything runs in-browser, with no persistent storage
  • Modular: Each feature is independently testable and documented
  • Accessible: Built with semantic HTML, keyboard navigation, and high contrast
  • Transparent: Open-source and auditable by anyone

Why We Built It

Most link validators are cloud-bound, slow, or invasive. This tool was born out of frustration with those limitations—and a desire to empower users with something fast, local, and trustworthy. It’s part of a broader mission to create privacy-first utilities that respect user intent and minimize friction.

FAQ

Is this tool part of a larger suite?

Yes. It’s one of several privacy-first tools under the AxelBase banner, designed for clarity and autonomy.

Can I contribute to the project?

Absolutely. Visit our GitHub repo to fork, audit, or suggest improvements.

Why avoid AI-based link checkers?

AI tools often rely on server-side inference and opaque models. We prefer rule-based clarity and local control.
